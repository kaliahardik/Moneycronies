Philosophy

Belief

Every investor has a worldview. Here's mine — the principles that guide every piece of research I write and every position I take.

01

Business First, Stock Second

I am not buying a ticker — I am buying a fractional ownership in a real enterprise. Understanding the business deeply always precedes any price discussion.

02

Margin of Safety is Non-Negotiable

The future is uncertain by definition. I only act when the price offers enough downside protection to survive being wrong about timing, magnitude, or both.

03

Patience is a Competitive Advantage

Most investors can't hold through a 30% drawdown without flinching. My edge isn't prediction — it's temperament. The turtle wins the race.

04

Moats Compound, Not Just Earnings

A business that earns 20% returns for 5 years is interesting. One that earns 15% returns for 25 years because competitors can't catch it — that's extraordinary.

05

Management Allocates Capital — Judge Them on That

A CEO who generates high returns on incremental capital is worth more than one who grows revenue. I study capital allocation decisions as carefully as financials.

06

Uncertainty is Not the Same as Risk

Risk is permanent loss of capital. Uncertainty is not knowing the path to a destination you can still reach. I welcome uncertainty; I avoid risk.

My Library

The books and texts that shaped how I think about businesses, people, and capital markets. Not a reading list — a worldview in print.

B

The Intelligent Investor

Benjamin Graham

The bible. Mr. Market, margin of safety, and the temperament required to do this right. I re-read chapters 8 and 20 every year.

Foundational
P

Poor Charlie's Almanack

Charles T. Munger

Multi-disciplinary thinking applied to investing and life. The latticework of mental models concept changed how I read every annual report since.

Mental Models
C

Competition Demystified

Bruce Greenwald & Judd Kahn

The clearest framework for identifying true competitive advantages versus temporary ones. Essential for any analyst who cares about moats.

Strategy
T

The Outsiders

William N. Thorndike Jr.

Eight unconventional CEOs who proved that capital allocation skill matters as much — if not more — than operational excellence.

Management
Q

Quality Investing

Lawrence A. Cunningham et al.

A practitioner's guide to finding and holding genuinely high-quality businesses. The section on return on capital and reinvestment dynamics is outstanding.

Valuation
M

Memos from Howard Marks

Howard Marks

Risk, cycles, psychology. The memos (freely available on Oaktree's site) are some of the most honest writing in all of finance.

Hi, I'm Hardik.

I spent seven years in investment banking and came away with one conviction: the best analytical work starts with deeply understanding the business, not just the transaction.

At J.P. Morgan and The Chesapeake Group, I worked on M&A deals across infrastructure, energy, and technology in India, Australia, and the US. Good training. Fast-moving. I started Moneycronies on the side because I wanted a place to think slowly, write clearly, and hold my own ideas accountable.

The turtle is deliberate. Patient, methodical, and willing to go further than the surface suggests. No hot takes, no quarter-to-quarter noise. Just trying to understand businesses better than the next person.

I'm currently finishing my MBA at Rotman, University of Toronto. If you're a fellow investor, someone in finance, or just someone who finds businesses genuinely interesting, I'd love to hear from you.
